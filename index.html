Richard P. Feynman

An image of Feynman sitting in front of a blackboard

May 11th 1918

Richard Feynman was born in Queens, New York.

1935-1939

He studied physics at MIT as an undergraduate. The results of his bachelor-thesis is today known as Hellmann-Feynman-Theorem.

1939-1943

Feynman visited Princeton University and worked as an assistant of John Wheeler.

WW2

During the Second World War Feynman he was part of the Manhattan-Project and thus he contributed to the first Atomic Bomb. During this time he discovered his passion for playing the Bongo's.

After WW2

He started shifting his focus to quantum electrodynamic
